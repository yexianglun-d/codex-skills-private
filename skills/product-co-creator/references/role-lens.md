# Role Lens

Use role lenses to make the co-creation output feel like a real product team and not a generic brainstorm.

## How to choose lenses

Always include a product manager lens. Then add 1 to 3 additional lenses that are closest to the domain.

Choose lenses that affect the product's logic, not just its branding.

## Common lenses

### Product manager

Focus on:

- user need
- scope
- prioritization
- tradeoffs
- roadmap

### Domain expert

Focus on:

- domain truth
- terminology
- professional constraints
- quality of guidance
- trustworthiness

### Operator

Focus on:

- repeatable flows
- handoff logic
- service burden
- backend process implications

### Growth lead

Focus on:

- activation
- return loop
- referral hooks
- upgrade moments

### Content strategist

Focus on:

- content structure
- education sequence
- messaging clarity
- repeat consumption

## Example: Fitness product

For a fitness recording mini program, a useful role stack is:

- product manager
- health coach
- senior trainer

This combination helps answer:

- what the user should record
- how training plans should be structured
- which feedback is useful without pretending to be medical diagnosis
- how progress should feel visible and motivating

## Safety rule

When a domain touches health, finance, law, or other regulated areas, use the expert lens to improve product shape, but do not fabricate regulated authority. Mark boundaries clearly and keep advice within safe product-design scope.
